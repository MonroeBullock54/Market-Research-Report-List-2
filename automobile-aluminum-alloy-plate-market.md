<p><p>The automobile aluminum alloy plate market is primarily divided into two production methods: cold rolling and hot rolling. Cold rolling involves passing aluminum alloy sheets through rollers at room temperature, resulting in improved surface finish and tighter tolerances, making it suitable for applications requiring precision. In contrast, hot rolling involves heating the aluminum before processing, enhancing workability and structural properties while allowing for thicker plates. Each method serves distinct automotive needs, impacting weight reduction and fuel efficiency in vehicle manufacturing.</p></p>
