<p><p>The Parkinson's Disease (PD) drugs market comprises several therapeutic classes aimed at managing symptoms. Dopamine agonists mimic dopamine effects, enhancing motor control. Monoamine oxidase inhibitors prevent dopamine breakdown, increasing its availability. Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors address cognitive decline by inhibiting the enzyme that breaks down acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter essential for memory. Glutamate inhibitors reduce excitotoxicity associated with PD, providing neuroprotective effects. Collectively, these drugs aim to improve quality of life and alleviate the motor and non-motor symptoms of Parkinson's Disease.</p></p>
